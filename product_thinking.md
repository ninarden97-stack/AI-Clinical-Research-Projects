# Product Thinking: AI for Clinical Research Operations

This repository explores how AI systems could support key operational workflows in clinical research and health technology environments.

Rather than focusing on model development alone, the projects emphasise **product design, workflow integration, and governance considerations** required for deploying AI in regulated healthcare settings.

---

# Core Product Principles

### 1. AI Should Augment Human Expertise

Clinical research is highly regulated and decisions often require expert review.  
AI systems should assist humans by:

- structuring complex information
- highlighting potential risks
- accelerating operational processes

Human validation layers are therefore included in each project.

---

### 2. Structured Outputs Enable Automation

Many healthcare workflows rely on unstructured documents such as:

- trial protocols
- safety reports
- regulatory submissions

AI can extract structured data from these sources, enabling:

- downstream analytics
- operational dashboards
- automated monitoring systems

Example outputs in this repository use structured JSON schemas.

---

### 3. Explainability and Transparency Are Critical

In regulated environments, AI outputs must be explainable.

Each prototype includes:

- confidence indicators
- key drivers for predictions
- human review flags

This enables responsible adoption in clinical operations.

---

# Example AI Product Opportunities

These examples illustrate potential AI-enabled systems that could improve clinical research operations and patient experience.

---

## 1. Clinical Trial Protocol Analyser

Problem:  
Clinical trial protocols are lengthy documents containing complex operational and regulatory information.

Potential AI solution:

- Extract structured metadata from protocols
- Identify key endpoints, eligibility criteria, and safety monitoring plans
- Support protocol review and trial setup

Impact:

- Reduced manual document review
- Faster trial setup timelines
- Improved data consistency

---

## 2. Adverse Event Signal Detection

Problem:  
Pharmacovigilance teams must monitor large volumes of safety reports to identify potential adverse event signals.

Potential AI solution:

- Detect patterns in adverse event reports
- Identify clusters of similar safety events
- Highlight emerging safety signals for expert review

Impact:

- Faster safety signal detection
- Improved pharmacovigilance monitoring
- Support for regulatory safety reporting

---

## 3. Clinical Trial Site Feasibility Predictor

Problem:  
Selecting clinical trial sites with strong recruitment potential is difficult and often based on limited historical insights.

Potential AI solution:

- Use historical site performance data
- Train machine learning models to predict recruitment success
- Identify risk factors affecting enrollment performance

Impact:

- Improved site selection decisions
- Reduced recruitment delays
- More efficient trial planning

---

## 4. AI eConsent Assistant

Problem:  
Informed consent documents are often long and difficult for patients to understand, which can affect comprehension and ethical participation in research.

Potential AI solution:

- Generate plain-language explanations of consent documents
- Provide interactive summaries of study procedures and risks
- Create comprehension questions to verify participant understanding
- Support human review to ensure regulatory compliance

Impact:

- Improved patient comprehension
- Better informed consent processes
- Increased patient engagement in clinical research

---

# Governance and Risk Considerations

Deploying AI in healthcare requires strong governance frameworks.

Key considerations include:

- Human-in-the-loop validation
- Regulatory compliance
- auditability of AI outputs
- bias monitoring
- patient privacy protection

These prototypes include design patterns that support responsible AI deployment.

---

# Purpose of This Repository

This repository demonstrates **AI product design concepts** for healthcare and clinical research environments.

The focus is on:

- workflow design
- structured outputs
- operational integration
- governance awareness

rather than production-level model development.
